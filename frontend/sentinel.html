    How it works

    Four steps from inbox
    to decided.

    Sentinel runs once a day (or on demand). Every inbound is scored, routed, and pressure-tested by the four-advisor panel. You wake up to a brief, not a backlog.

    01
    Ingest
    Connector rollout starts with waitlist users. Gmail, Slack, and Teams onboarding happens in phases — Sentinel never sends on your behalf.
    02
    Score
    Every message gets a priority (P0–P3), a category (Legal, Finance, Ops…), and a recommended action (Reply, Schedule, Archive).
    03
    Debate
    Strategy, Finance, Technology and a Contrarian advisor each weigh in. Blind spots are surfaced from your Fourseat Memory.
    04
    Brief
    One clean Markdown brief lands in your inbox. Forward it, paste it into Slack, or archive it. That’s the whole ritual.

    Questions

    The honest
    answers.

    Does Sentinel read all my email?

    Only the inbound you haven’t archived. It ignores sent mail, drafts, and anything in folders you exclude. You can revoke access any time from Google or your workspace admin.

    Can Sentinel reply or send messages?

    No. Sentinel is read-only by design. It surfaces what needs a reply and drafts suggested language — you stay the sender.

    Where does the data live?

    Threads are processed in-session and summaries are stored encrypted in your Fourseat workspace. Nothing is used for training and nothing leaves your workspace unless you forward it.

    What if the AI gets it wrong?

    Every verdict ships with a confidence score and the full advisor debate. You can one-tap reprioritise or mark items resolved. Corrections feed your Memory so next week’s brief is sharper.
